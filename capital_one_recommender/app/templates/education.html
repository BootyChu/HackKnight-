Financial Literacy Terms

APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

The cost of borrowing money on a credit card, shown as a yearly percentage. If you don’t pay your balance in full each month, APR determines how much interest you owe.

Credit Utilization

The percentage of your credit limit that you’re using. Keeping it below 30% helps maintain a good credit score.

Rewards Rates

The percentage of cashback, points, or miles you earn on purchases. Example: A 2% cashback card gives you $2 for every $100 spent.

Hidden Fees

Extra charges that aren’t always obvious, like annual fees, late payment fees, and foreign transaction fees.

Late Fees

A penalty charged if you miss your payment due date. It can also hurt your credit score.

Interest Charges

Extra money you pay if you don’t pay your full credit card balance on time. It’s based on your card’s APR.

Credit Score

A number (300-850) that shows how responsible you are with credit. A high score makes it easier to get loans and lower interest rates.
